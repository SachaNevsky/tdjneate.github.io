About

Hi, I am Tim - a Research Fellow and (sometimes) Visiting Lecturer in the Centre for Human-Computer Interaction Design in the Department of Computer Science at City, University of London. I work on the EPSRC funded INCA Project, which aims to enable people with Aphasia in engaging in the act of digital content creation.

With my work I aim to enable richer and more equitable experiences with technology; to enable end-users in the design of the technologies they use and to promote human flourishing and prosperity. My work generally fits into the following labels: human-computer interaction, media technology, tangibles, multi-device interaction, accessibility. For more information, check out my published work. Or, look at some selected press coverage for a more high-level perspective.

I got my PhD from the FIT Lab, Department of Computer Science, at Swansea University and my masters and undergraduate degrees at the Department of Electronics at the University of York. I have previously worked at Fraunhofer IIS's Audio Labs and as a Research Officer at the FIT Lab at Swansea.
